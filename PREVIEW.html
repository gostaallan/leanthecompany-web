THE PROBLEM

You don't have a people problem. You have an invisibility problem.

"Where there is no standard, there can be no kaizen." — Taiichi Ohno

Every factory and every office runs on three kinds of waste that nobody writes down because nobody can see them. Until you draw them.

01 · Chaos

Nobody knows the real process.

Ask five people to describe the order-to-ship flow and you'll get five different maps. The one on the wall is three years and two ERPs out of date. Decisions get made against fiction.

02 · Handoffs

Handoffs eat more time than the work.

The machine cycle is 40 seconds. The order spends 11 days between people, inboxes and "waiting for clarification." Speed lives in the white space, not the value-added steps.

03 · Shadow

Half the work isn't in any system.

Senior operators and admins run parallel spreadsheets, side-channel chats and heroic workarounds. When they leave, the knowledge leaves. When they stay, the knowledge never scales.

ABOUT THE FOUNDER

Three industries. One repeating problem. That's why this exists.

GS

Gösta Seuranen.

Engineer, operator, coach. Malmö ↔ Shanghai. 25+ years on the shop floor before I wrote a line of code.

Ten years at Tetra Pak. Five at Efeso Consulting. Twelve more as an Efeso contractor running WCOM and TPM rollouts at Stora Enso, SCA, BillerudKorsnäs, Oatly, Galderma and Nobia — Sweden, Finland, the Netherlands, the United States, China, UK. Different industries — the same three problems on every shop floor.

I started at Tetra Pak in 1999 and spent a decade inside one of the world's most disciplined food-packaging machinery businesses. Lean wasn't an initiative there — it was how the lifts worked, how meetings ended, how line-side parts were laid out. That's where I learned the trade.

From 2009 to 2014 I was at Efeso Consulting, running lean transformations across Europe and Asia. During those same years I also took operator stints at FrieslandCampina and Heineken via Asia Pacific Breweries. Same period, two angles — client-side and consultant-side. The same three failure patterns showed up in every diagnostic.

From 2014 onwards I've worked as an Efeso contractor leading WCOM and TPM rollouts — Stora Enso across Sweden, Finland and China; SCA and BillerudKorsnäs in Sweden; Oatly across Sweden, the Netherlands, the US and China (they hired me direct for a stretch); Galderma in pharma; Nobia in kitchens, UK and Sweden. Twelve more years of the same diagnostics, refined into a method I could run in any plant in any sector.

Consultants solve this one plant at a time, at €2,000 a day, and the moment they leave the gravity reasserts. I wanted a tool a 26-year-old shift supervisor could open on a Sunday evening, map her own line, and walk in Monday with a better standard. That is what LeanTheCompany is.
